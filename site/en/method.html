Method

How IRBIS
works.

From first contact to a live product in 3 weeks. No manager in between, no 40-slide proposal.

01 · First contact

One message.
No forms.

WhatsApp or email. No briefing form, no platform signup, no automated pre-qualification. You send a message describing the problem, I reply.

Within 24 hours we book a quick call: 30 minutes max. The goal is to understand the problem, calibrate the scope, and see whether working together makes sense.

If it doesn't make sense, I say so. Straight to the point, no runaround.

I don't take every project. If your problem isn't something I can solve well, whether by specialty, timing, or fit, you leave the call knowing that, not after three weeks of proposals.

02 · The diagnosis

One document.
No surprises.

After the call: a written brief, delivered within 48 hours. A document made for you to reach a decision, not to fill a meeting.

The brief covers:

  • 01Problem identified: how I understood what needs solving
  • 02Proposed approach: what I will build and why
  • 03Stack: the technologies, platforms and tools involved
  • 04Timeline: concrete dates, not vague estimates
  • 05Price: the final number, no hidden pieces

No negotiation theater. The price reflects the scope described. If the scope changes, the price changes. And that conversation happens before any work starts.

03 · The execution

Payment confirmed.
Work starts.

One upfront payment. Or 50/50 for larger projects. No 12-month installment plans, no "we start once the contract is signed in triplicate".

Payment confirmed, work starts the next business day.

Weekly updates on WhatsApp. No status meetings. No progress decks.

Every week you get a message with what got done, what comes next, and whether any decision is waiting on you. Async communication by default: I work, you don't need to be available around the clock.

1 revision round included in the original scope. Major changes of direction: the kind that changes the problem being solved, not just how it gets solved, trigger a new brief before any execution.

04 · The delivery

Deployed to production.
You own everything.

The project goes live. Not to staging, not to "final validation before go-live". To production, with domain, with SSL, with everything working.

Along with the deploy: handoff documentation, enough for you (or any dev) to understand what was built and how to change it without needing me for every small tweak.

  • →30 days of post-delivery support for bugs in what was shipped
  • →No hosting lock-in. You pick where to host. Or keep the infra I set up and pay the provider directly
  • →The code is yours. Repository, domain, service accounts: everything in your name or transferred to you

I don't build dependency. You don't need IRBIS to maintain what was built.
