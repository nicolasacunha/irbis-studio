A family of its own.

Despite the name, sweetpotatoes belong to a totally different family from the English or Irish potato — a sweet-tasting root vegetable full of goodness to fill your day.

The growers

Incorporated by growers, for growers.

ASPG began informally in 2003, as a group of producers coming together for shared benefit. In May 2007 it was formally incorporated — and it has looked after the interests of Australian sweetpotato growers ever since.

The association represents producers to local, state and Commonwealth government, works with the Queensland Department of Agriculture and Fisheries on trials and production practices, and maintains close links with Louisiana State University — the premier sweetpotato research institution in the United States.

Biosecurity

Keeping exotic pests out of Australian paddocks.

As the Peak Industry Body for sweetpotatoes, ASPG has signed the national Emergency Plant Pest Response Deed with Plant Health Australia — committing growers to help eradicate exotic pest incursions, and securing support for growers when they are affected.

Grower levies also fund the Sweetpotato Biosecurity Plan and targeted science on priority pests.
